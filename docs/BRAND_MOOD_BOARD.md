# Rise With The Tribe Brand Mood Board

## Brand Idea

Rise With The Tribe should feel like a disciplined community climbing together: energetic, accountable, human, and premium without becoming cold or corporate.

Visual board:

`docs/marketing-assets/rise-with-the-tribe-brand-board.svg`

## Visual Pillars

- Rising path: upward movement, sunrise, peaks, finish-line momentum.
- Tribe signal: grouped shapes, shared arcs, member-to-member accountability.
- Training energy: bold contrast, compact UI, progress-first composition.
- Warm grit: effort and consistency, not luxury wellness or generic gym hype.

## Typography Direction

- Product UI: compact, strong sans-serif with clear numerals.
- Campaign headlines: heavy uppercase or title case, short lines, high contrast.
- Support copy: plain, direct, encouraging, no hype-heavy fitness cliches.
- Avoid thin decorative fonts, script fonts, and long all-caps paragraphs.

## Image Direction

- Prefer real effort: training logs, challenge boards, sweat, steps, groups, hands, timer moments.
- Use warm highlights against dark training-space contrast.
- Show people as capable and in motion, not posed like stock fitness ads.
- Keep social content app-first: challenge name, progress signal, invitation, proof.

The mark uses a sunrise, peak/path, and community geometry to show people rising together. Keep the icon text-free for all app-library and favicon contexts.

## Usage Rules

- Keep the brand high-contrast: dark base plus teal/coral/amber energy.
- Use green only for completion or growth signals, not as the dominant brand color.
- Do not switch into beige wellness, purple tech gradients, or generic bodybuilding visuals.
- Do not add tiny text inside icons or social graphics where it will collapse on mobile.
- For release assets, keep Universal Links and app icons aligned with `risewiththetribe.app`.

## Content Tone

- Direct, accountable, grounded.
- Invite action without pressure.
- Celebrate consistency over transformation promises.
- Avoid medical claims, guaranteed results, shame, or fake scarcity.
